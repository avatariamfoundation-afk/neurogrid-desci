# DAO_CONFLICT_OF_INTEREST_POLICY.md  
**Conflict Identification, Disclosure & Mitigation Framework**

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes mandatory rules to **identify, disclose, manage, and mitigate conflicts of interest (COI)** within the NeuroGrid DAO.

Its objective is to:
- Preserve governance integrity
- Prevent undue influence
- Protect patients, contributors, and public trust
- Ensure regulatory and ethical defensibility

---

## 2. Core Principle

> **Governance legitimacy depends on impartial decision-making.  
Conflicts must be disclosed, not concealed.**

No DAO member may participate in decisions where personal interest compromises objectivity.

---

## 3. Scope

This policy applies to:
- DAO members
- Core contributors
- Review committee members
- Emergency power holders
- Any party with voting or proposal authority

---

## 4. Definition of Conflict of Interest

A conflict of interest exists when an individual’s:
- Financial interest
- Professional role
- Personal relationship
- External obligation

**could reasonably influence** their judgment or actions within the DAO.

Perception of conflict is sufficient to trigger disclosure.

---

## 5. Types of Conflicts

### 5.1 Financial Conflicts
- Token holdings tied to proposal outcomes
- Equity, grants, or compensation from affected entities
- Revenue-sharing arrangements

### 5.2 Professional Conflicts
- Employment or advisory roles
- Consulting contracts
- Research sponsorships

### 5.3 Research & Academic Conflicts
- Dataset ownership
- Model authorship
- Publication or citation incentives

### 5.4 Governance Conflicts
- Dual DAO roles
- Concentrated voting power
- Emergency authority overlaps

### 5.5 Personal Conflicts
- Family or close relationships
- Personal benefit or harm exposure

---

## 6. Mandatory Disclosure Requirements

All covered individuals must:
- Disclose conflicts **before participation**
- Update disclosures when circumstances change
- Declare conflicts per proposal

Failure to disclose constitutes a governance violation.

---

## 7. Disclosure Mechanism

Disclosures must include:
- Nature of the conflict
- Affected proposal or domain
- Financial or non-financial interest
- Duration and scope

Disclosures are:
- Logged publicly
- Linked to proposal records
- Permanently auditable

---

## 8. Conflict Severity Levels

| Level | Description |
|-----|------------|
| Low | Indirect or minimal influence |
| Moderate | Potential influence |
| High | Direct financial or decision impact |
| Critical | Exclusive or controlling interest |

Severity determines mitigation requirements.

---

## 9. Mitigation Measures

Depending on severity, mitigation may include:
- Mandatory abstention
- Voting weight reduction
- Removal from review committees
- Independent third-party review
- Temporary suspension of authority

Critical conflicts require **full recusal**.

---

## 10. Voting Restrictions

- Conflicted members may not vote on affected proposals
- Delegated votes are also restricted
- Quorum calculations exclude conflicted parties

Circumvention is prohibited.

---

## 11. Proposal Sponsorship Rules

A proposer with a conflict must:
- Declare conflict at submission
- Accept heightened scrutiny
- Submit to independent review

Undisclosed conflicted proposals are invalid.

---

## 12. Emergency Powers & Conflicts

Emergency authority holders must:
- Disclose conflicts immediately
- Transfer authority if conflicted
- Document actions taken

Emergency action does not override disclosure obligations.

---

## 13. Enforcement & Sanctions

Violations may result in:
- Proposal nullification
- Governance sanctions
- Loss of voting rights
- Removal from DAO roles
- Public disclosure of violations

Severe violations may trigger regulatory escalation.

---

## 14. Appeals Process

Accused parties may:
- Submit written response
- Request independent review
- Appeal sanctions via governance vote

Appeals do not stay enforcement unless approved.

---

## 15. Transparency & Recordkeeping

All COI disclosures are:
- Publicly accessible
- Time-stamped
- Immutable
- Linked to governance decisions

Redactions are not permitted except for legally protected data.

---

## 16. Regulatory Alignment

This policy aligns with:
- Medical ethics standards
- Research integrity frameworks
- DAO governance best practices
- Regulatory expectations for clinical AI oversight

---

## 17. Policy Amendments

Changes to this policy require:
- Class V proposal
- Supermajority approval
- Public disclosure

---

## 18. Binding Status

This policy is:
- Mandatory
- Enforceable
- Governance-critical
